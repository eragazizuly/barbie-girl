About Barbie in the 1960s

Barbie, the iconic doll created by Ruth Handler, became a cultural phenomenon in the 1960s. During this decade, Barbie's wardrobe, hairstyles, and accessories reflected the trends and styles of the time, capturing the imagination of millions of children around the world.

Fashion Highlights

In the 1960s, Barbie's fashion mirrored the elegance of the era, featuring chic dresses, mod outfits, and bold prints. She embraced trends like go-go boots, pillbox hats, and cat-eye sunglasses, showcasing the evolution of women's style in the decade.

Collections

Collectors call the Turn N Twist Barbie TNT. She was the first of what later became known as the Mod Barbie Era from 1967 to 1973. The doll looks a little different because she has rooted eyelashes, long straight hair, bendable legs, and her waist can be turned, hence the name. The Twist N Turn Barbie was the most popular in the 1960s, according to Mattel. Both Barbie and Ken were released as astronauts in 1965, reflecting the enthusiasm in the U.S. around the space program. Mattel was on the right track.
